How to use GWACs

The conformed SCRIPTS BPAs contain the scope, technical requirements, and information regarding pricing, order types, roles and responsibilities, and applicable parts of the Federal Acquisition Regulation. State, local, tribal, and territorial governments may be eligible to use Cooperative Purchasing.

  1. Plan the Acquisition

     Conduct market research and acquisition planning. 

    • Competition Requirements:
      • Below micro-purchase threshold - order from any BPA awardee
      • Above micro-purchase, below simplified acquisition threshold - fair opportunity to all BPA awardees unless Limited Sources justification applies
      • Above simplified acquisition threshold - must post RFQ on eBuy to all applicable BPA awardees.
    • Set-asides: If your requirement meets ONLY the Small Business suite’s five minimum risk categories, restrict competition to Small Business BPA awardees. Otherwise, compete among all SCRIPTS BPA Holders.
    • Pricing: Fair and reasonable pricing is established at the BPA level. You may negotiate additional order-level discounts above the simplified acquisition threshold.
    • Vendors: Use the vendor lists to identify SCRIPTS awardees. To view a vendor’s GSA contract award information, visit their profile on eLibrary. 

  2. Define Requirements

     Develop Statement of Work or Performance Work Statement.

  3. Issue RFQ 

    For orders above the simplified acquisition threshold, post your RFQ on eBuy for BPA holders to respond. Ordering activities below that threshold may use eBuy but are not required to do so.

    You can use eBuy submit questions or issue solicitations directly to our SCRIPTS vendors. Include any socioeconomic set-asides and special delivery, asset management, or security requirements. Only authorized SCRIPTS vendors can view and respond to requests on eBuy. To limit competition to SCRIPTS BPA holders only...

    To place government purchase card orders, use GSAAdvantage!  You can identify and filter for products available through SCRIPTS. 

  4. Evaluate quotes

    Assess quotes based on order-level evaluation criteria.

  5. Award the order 

    Document award with BPA Holder information. Remember to always follow your agency's acquisition procedures.

    • Alliant 2 Ordering Guide — A guide for ordering and administering task orders under the Alliant 2 GWAC.
    • SBGWAC Ordering Guide — A guide for ordering and administering task orders under the Small Business GWACs, including 8(a) STARS III, Polaris and VETS 2.
    • Defense Contracting Officer’s Representative Handbook
    • Guidance on creating 508 compliant IT solicitations

  6. Administer the order

    Monitor performance and close out order. Contracting officers must submit complete reports on required contract actions within three business days after a contract is awarded, or in accordance with the guidance from your agency. 

    The Federal Acquisition Regulation requires customer agencies to collect and report data on their procurements to the Federal Procurement Data System, which refers to GWACs as indefinite delivery vehicles or IDVs. Most information is automatically updated based on the user’s account and the characteristics of the parent GWAC when creating or modifying an award.
